About Me

I grew up in Tidewater Virginia and earned a double-major in Computer Science and History at the University of Virginia. I then worked in I/T in the DC area for three years before starting grad school at North Carolina State University. I earned my PhD studying Software Security and Software Engineering under Dr. Laurie Williams and Dr. Marcelo D'Amorim. I am currently a Teaching Assistant Professor at the University of Virginia.

Interests

I spent most of my industry career as a software tester. Right now I am focused on Software Security Metrics and their use in the Software Supply Chain. My previous projects include examining Security Testing Techniques, understanding and extracting requirements from Security Regulations, Behavior Driven Development, and work with Differential Privacy. At the end of the day, if it involves computers I am probably at least a little curious.

Contact
